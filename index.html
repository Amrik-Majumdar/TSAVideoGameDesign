THE LAST BROADCAST

1987

You are a radio DJ at Station WKLS-AM, hosting the final night before the corporate buyout.

The transmitter is failing. The board wants you to keep it professional, play the hits, and hand over the keys at sunrise.

But you have 12 vinyl records left, and the phone won't stop ringing. People calling with their stories—heartbreaks, regrets, small victories, desperate prayers.

Each caller needs something different. Choose the right song, and you might change someone's night. Choose wrong, and they fade into static.

The transmitter drops every hour. You can boost it with upbeat songs—but that might not be what the caller needs. Do you save the station, or do you save the people?
